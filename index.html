Rules and How to Play

Goal

Empty your hand before the computer by playing cards into valid table groups.

Valid groups

  • Sets: 3 or 4 cards with the same rank.
  • Runs: 3 or more cards of the same suit in sequence. A run can be longer than 4 cards, and can wrap from K to A.

Your turn

  • Play at least one card, then end your turn while every table group is valid.
  • You may rearrange existing table cards as long as the whole table remains valid before ending.
  • Drag a single card onto a group, select cards and use table actions, or make a new group.
  • If you cannot play, draw. If a move exists, drawing is blocked.

Table actions

  • New group: Put the selected cards into their own new table group.
  • Add to a meld: Select cards, then click a meld to add them and automatically sort the result.
  • Take back: Return selected cards you played this turn to your hand.
  • Restore turn: Undo all of your changes and return the table, deck, and your hand to how they were at the start of your turn.

Computer difficulty

  • Easy: Plays one straightforward move and does not look for table splits.
  • Medium: Plays multiple moves and can use simple splits.
  • Hard: Uses the full table-repartition search.

Card markers

The small green dot on a table card means you played that card this turn, so it can still be selected and taken back before you end your turn.

Help

Use Show move to check for a possible play. It highlights the involved cards and describes the move without making it for you.

Manipulation example

If the table has 4-5-6-7-8 of hearts and 8-8-8, and you hold 6 of hearts, you could split the heart run into 4-5-6 and 6-7-8, leaving the set of 8s intact. More involved turns can split multiple existing groups and recombine their cards, as long as every group left on the table has at least 3 cards and is a valid set or run.
